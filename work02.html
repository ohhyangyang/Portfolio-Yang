X-Book

A BOOK EXCHANGE PLATFORM


X-Book is a platform that enables users to upload their books and exchange them with other users.


X-Book is developed with the SSR technology (Server Side Rendering) and I pair programmed with the amazing teammate Juliane Huber, it's framed during the second module of Ironhack Bootcamp, it quickly leveled up my backend skill.

Experience

The first highlight of this experience is the rigorous backend logical thinking. We applied a very complex logic to build up a database relation and realize the exchange functionality between users, which not only required both of us to have a sharp logical thought, but also tested us the ability to communicate, and in order to achieve a more fluid and intuitive communication, we drew the amount of logic diagram to help us to understand the context.




The second highlight is our outstanding team. Actually I didn't know Juliane very much when I invited her to work together, but my intuition told me we'd work well together, and the result proved I was right. Due to the COVID, and also to dedicate more time to our project and work more efficiently, we decided to work together at each other's house, after the intense work, more than often we cooked for each other and shared food at the terrace‚≠ê.



User stories:

  • Signup: The page where the user can sign up to the book exchange platform
  • Login: The page where the user can log in to his/her profile
  • Profile page: Shows the profile (username, photo) of the user, the book borrow notifications, the books he/she is offering and the exchanged books
  • Edit user: User can change his/her profile
  • Book Library: Shows the "library" with the books (information displayed, reviews & co.) offered and already exchanged, filter options (categories), you can borrow the books there
  • Add books: User can add additional books
  • Delete book: Of course you can remove books from your library

What I learnt

1. Communication is the key

Let's confess, teamwork is hard, and most of the teamwork is not so enjoyable, and always comes with a high failure rate, but once it works, it will bring amazing power to the project.

2. Nothing is clearer than drawing it vividly on paper

When working in a complex logical system, it is very easy to get lost, and the easiest and most intuitive way to solve this issue is to draw it out, this is a very important lesson for my career.
